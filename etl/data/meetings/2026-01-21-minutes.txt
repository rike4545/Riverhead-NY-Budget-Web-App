VII.  Public Hearings 
Public Hearing called at 6:14 pm 
Greg Bergman gave an overview of the proposed code amendment.  
Gary Sherica spoke opposed to cottages due to their transient nature, threatening 
quality of life. Lives in adjoining neighborhood that may be affected.  
Laura Jens Smith spoke in favor of the concept of viability of amendments, but 
emphasized the use of transient lodging, and the density written in the code may 
become an unwanted outcome. Also spoke on behalf of the Jamesport Civic 
Association and passed up correspondence to be made part of the record.  
Amanda Grahm wanted to know who can use these cottages, and wanted 
clarification.  
Barbara Blass offered suggestions to clarify and understand the newly adopted 
Comprehensive Plan. 
Sid Bail WRCivic Ass. It said that the comp plan stated 600 square feet was 
adopted, and the new definition calls for 1200 square feet. 
Kerry Fagurniak wanted to know specifics about roadways and ownership. 
Graig Vassey spoke in favor of cottages and the beauty of the golf courses in our 
area.  
ZOOM — Kathy McGraw, not opposed to cottages, doesn't like the connection of 
cottages in lieu of separate, which will resemble a hotel.  
ZOOM—  Ellen Hoyle, spoke that 1200 sq.feet may be too large. 
ZOOM — Joan Ceryn — definition of cottages needs to be tightly defined, it is too 
vague in its current form.  
ZOOM — Ron Hariri — additional housing around courses what impact on the tax 
assessment. 
ZOOM — Kathy McGraw a second time, asked whether the definition currently on 
record remains.  
Closed at 7:35 and held open till Feb 2nd for written comment.

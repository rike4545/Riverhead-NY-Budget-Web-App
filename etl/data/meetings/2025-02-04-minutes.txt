RESOLUTION CALLING PUBLIC HEARING REGARDING A CAPITAL 
IMPROVEMENT FOR THE PROPOSED ELECTRICAL UPGRADES AT PLANT NO. 7 
RIVERHEAD WATER DISTRICT PROJECT NO. 16 
 
Councilman Kern offered the following resolution, 
which was seconded by Councilwoman Merrifield 
 
WHEREAS, Riverhead Water District Plant No.7, located on Fresh Pond Avenue 
in Calverton, consists of two wells with a total approved capacity of approximately 3.46 
million gallons and contains two of the District’s five high zone wells; and 
 
WHEREAS, Plant No. 7 was originally constructed in the late 1980’s and 
included two wells and a main treatment building which included the electrical system, 
in 1995 one of the wells was abandoned and replaced; and  
 
WHEREAS, a map and plan dated December 2024 has been prepared by H2M, 
consulting engineers to the Riverhead Water District, detailing the need to replace and 
upgrade the electrical components including replacing the existing motor control cente r 
and power feeders; and 
 
WHEREAS, the map and plan are available for review and inspection at the 
Office of the Riverhead Town Clerk, 4 West 2 nd Street, Riverhead, New York, during 
normal business hours; and 
 
WHEREAS, said revised map and plan call for th e demolition of existing motor 
control center including incoming electrical service, transformer, starters, service 
entrance, and all panels; the upgrade of primary service lines from 600A to 800A 
including new transformer; the installation of new site sec ondary feeder lines; 
installation of Variable Frequency Drive on each well pump with line and load reactors 
as required; new LED interior and exterior lighting; update the electrical controls and 
fully integrate with the District’s controls system; replace ment of electric well feeders; 
removal of existing generator, piping and fuel tank; and, installation of a new diesel 
generator for emergency power to cover entire site with above grade fuel tank, 
monitoring system, and automatic transfer switch. The estim ated project cost to 
implement the rehabilitation of the electrical systems at Plant No. 7 is $2,350,000.00. 
These costs also include engineering, bonds and insurance, administration and 
contingencies; and  
 
WHEREAS, the costs associated with this project shall be borne by the 
Riverhead Water District and it is anticipated the project shall be funded through the 
issuance of municipal bonds; and  
 
WHEREAS, the Town Board desires to call a public hearing on the adoption of a 
final order concerning the petition.

TB Resolution 2025-169 
 
ESTABLISHES THE TOWN OF RIVERHEAD HISPANIC DEVELOPMENT, 
EMPOWERMENT AND EDUCATION COMMITTEE AND APPOINTS MEMBERS 
 
 offered the following resolution, 
which was seconded by  
 
WHEREAS, the Town of Riverhead is a diverse community evidenced by the 
high percentage of school enrollment from the Hispanic and Latino community; and 
WHEREAS, while many quality of life issues are already addressed by the Town 
of Riverhead, through its Police Department, Code Enforcement, Community 
Development Agency and a host of other departments and town committees, the Town 
seeks to expand its efforts and create a Hispanic Development, Empowerment and 
Education Committee to develop and recommend strategies to abate and  address the 
issues related to and necessary to improve quality of life of Hispanic members of the 
community and build a bridge between the Town government and the Hispanic 
community to better serve everyone and represent everyone ; and 
WHEREAS, the members  of the Hispanic Development, Empowerment and 
Education Committee shall serve at the pleasure of the Town Board and without 
compensation with the purpose to act as an advisory committee to the Town Board 
making recommendations related to improvement of qua lity of life and tools/methods to 
improve communication and services to better serve the Hispanic community; and  
WHEREAS, the Hispanic Development, Empowerment and Education 
Committee shall serve at the pleasure of the Town Board. There shall be seven members 
appointed by the Town Board and all members shall have voting rights. The members shall 
be appointed to serve staggered terms of one year and two years. A member filling a 
vacancy shall fill the unexpired term. The Town Board shall appoint a member of  the 
Hispanic Development, Empowerment and Education Committee  as Chairperson to 
serve for a two year period and shall be responsible for scheduling of meetings and agendas, 
record attendance, notice the Town Board on vacancies, and such other matters related to its 
goals and purpose and appoint a Vice Chairperson to act in the absence of and in place of 
the Chairperson to a two year term; and          
WHEREAS, the Hispanic Development, Empowerment and Education 
Committee shall meet once a month at Town Hall or such other designated location. The 
Chairperson or Vice Chairperson, as the case may be, may schedule the regular/monthly 
meetings and such subcommittee meetings deemed appropriate. All of the seven members 
shall be required to attend 75% of all regular meetings each calendar year and may not miss 
three consecutive regular meetings. At the end of each calendar year (January 1 to 
December 31), the Chairperson shall notify the Town Board for compliance with the above, 
together with any recommendation to replace membership for failure to comply with 
attendance requirements, resignations, or such other vacancies; and

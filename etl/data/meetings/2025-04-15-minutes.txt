BUDGET AMENDMENT FOR CAPITAL PROJECT #72306- VETERAN’S MEMORIAL 
PARK SANITATION SYSTEM 
 
Councilman Rothwell offered the following resolution, 
which was seconded by Councilman Kern 
 
WHEREAS, by Resolution #2019-397 adopted on May 22, 2019, the Town 
Board did authorize and approve a Community Benefits Agreement between Riverhead 
Solar I (S Power) and the Town; and 
 
WHEREAS, by Resolution #2020-649 adopted on December 15, 2020, the Town 
Board did  authorize and approve a Utility Easement by and between LI Solar 
Generation (NextEra) and the Town in the amount of $450,000.00 and by Resolution 
#2020-646 the Town did authorize and approve a Community Benefits Agreement 
between LI Solar Generation (NextEra) and the Town; and   
  
WHEREAS, pursuant to the S Power Community Benefits Agreement funding 
category Promotion and Protection of Agriculture and Open Space Initiative, funds may 
be expended to achieve or be in furtherance of preservation of farmland, open 
space...establishment of nature preserves, preservation of public access to lands for 
public use; and 
 
WHEREAS, pursuant to Resolution #2019-858 adopted on December 3, 2019, 
the Town approved an expenditure for Capital Project #72228 to improve Sharpers Hill 
property in an amount not to exceed $81,600.00 with the expenditure of S-Power 
Community Benefit Funds from the Protection of Agriculture and Open Space Initiative 
funding category; and 
  
 WHEREAS, pursuant to Resolution #2020-445 a budget transfer was established 
for the sanitary system to be built at Veteran’s Memorial Park coming from the NextEra 
Community Benefit Agreement under the funding category of Promotion and 
Enhancement of Community Health and Welfare Initiative, through which economic 
development of and creation of additional and improvements of existing recreational 
facilities located at Veteran’s Memorial Park will be achieved through the infrastructural 
improvement described herein; and 
 
WHEREAS, during construction of the sanitary system referenced in Resolution 
#2020-445, change orders with increased costs for and related to the shift of the 
sanitary system approximately 50 feet to the east to provide sufficient clearance to the 
proposed water district 10” water main extension to run southerly between Line Road 
and the sanitary system;  the existing grade of the revised location of the sanitary 
system was found to be 5 feet higher than the original location and, as such, required 
the excavation and removal of an additional 782 cubic yards of material; and finally, the 
existing manhole (#2) at the south end of the ball fields which was installed in 2008 and 
8.1

between the ball fields from manhole #1 to manhole #2 to be removed and reinstalled

AND TOWNS TO IMPOSE CERTAIN ZONING REQUIREMENTS” 
 
Councilwoman Waski offered the following resolution, 
which was seconded by Councilman Rothwell 
 
WHEREAS, the Town of Riverhead has a long -standing history of independent 
and autonomous governance an d making decisions that best serve the needs of its 
residents and community; and 
 
 WHEREAS, a succession of past Town Boards and the present Town Board is 
committed to protecting the unique character and interests of our community, including 
our agricultur al history and heritage, preservation of our open space, unique 
environmental areas, farmland and our bucolic and rural vistas; and   
 
 WHEREAS, the Town of Riverhead has carefully considered the potential 
impacts of New York State Assembly Bill A2586/Sena te Bill S00188 introduced during 
the 2025 -2026 Legislative Session titled "Limits The Authority Of Cities, Villages And 
Towns To Impose Certain Zoning Requirements” and specifically its provisions 
prohibiting the Town of Riverhead from establishing a minim um lot size of  more than 
five thousand square feet if a lot has access to sewer and water infrastructure and 
establishing a minimum lot size of more than  twenty  thousand  square feet in any area 
within the boundaries of the Town of Riverhead; and  
 
 WHEREAS, the Town of Riverhead emphatically opposes Assembly Bill 
A2586/Senate Bill S00188 and the effort to undermine autonomous governance as to 
local matters, and specifically, the Town’s authority to adopt local zoning regulations as 
set forth in New York  State’s “enabling” laws: Town Law, and usurp the Town’s 
Municipal Home Rule Law and the Statute of Local Governments independent power to 
adopt, amend and repeal zoning regulations; and 
 WHEREAS, the provisions of Town Law, Municipal Home Rule Law and the  
Statute of Local Governments confer upon the Town of Riverhead’s independent power 
to adopt zoning laws which are enacted to promote the health, safety, and general 
welfare of this community, our community, the Town of Riverhead; and 
 WHEREAS, it is well recognized and stated in the laws above and stated clearly 
and distinctly in Town Law § 272-a. that “[a]mong the most important powers and duties 
granted by the legislature to a town government is the authority and responsibility to 
undertake town comprehe nsive planning and to regulate land use for the purpose of 
protecting the public health, safety and general welfare” and to make “[s]ignificant 
decisions and actions affecting the immediate and long -range protection, enhancement, 
growth and development of the state and its communities are made by local

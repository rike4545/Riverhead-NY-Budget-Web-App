VIII.  Public Hearings 
 ● 6:00 PM - Public Hearing - Special Permit Kar-McVeigh Restaurant Expansion  
Atty. Kim Judd, representing the applicant, presented the special permit and the 
reasons surrounding it.  
New owners were introduced and gave their model for business to fine dining and 
no catering events, different from the previous usages.  
Frank Mangiorno, a neighbor, spoke of past problems with previous ownership. 
Questioned lights and expansion of the business. 
Atty. Charles Cuddy explained his role in the special permit.  
Phil Barnados from Jamesport spoke on the expansion of pre-conforming use. 
The public hearing was closed and held open for written comments till September 
26th. 
 
  
 ● 6:10 PM - Public Hearing - Amend Chapter 289, Art. IV, Parking, Standing and 
Stopping  
Town Atty. Erik Howard gave an overview on amendments for parking and 
stopping on Youngs Ave.  
No comment from the public. Hearing closed and held open for written comment till 
September 26th. 
 ● 6:15 PM - Public Hearing - Amend Chapter 213, Art I, Bicycles  
TA Howard gave an overview of the amendment. Bicycles on CR 58 sidewalks.  
No Public Comment. Hearing closed and held open for written comment till 
September 26th. 
 ● 6:20 PM - Public Hearing - Amend Chapter 289, Art.I, Terminology  
TA Howard gave an overview of the amendment. No Public Comment. The hearing 
was closed and held open for public comment till September 26th.  
 ● 6:25 PM - Public Hearing - Amend Chapter 213. Art.II, Electric Scooters  
TA Howard gave an overview of the amendment. Electric Scooters and bicycles. 
Safety measures to mirror NYS VTL. No Public Comment. The hearing was closed 
and held open for written comment till September 26th.

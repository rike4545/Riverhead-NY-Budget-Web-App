APPENDIX B 
 
FEES AND EXPENSES 
 
 
The fees for our services for capital project financings will not exceed the following: Serial 
Bonds - $9,500 for each bond issue plus $0.60 per $1,000 thereafter, prior to the reduction of 
premium; Refunding Serial Bonds - $15,500 for each bond issue plus $1.75 per $1,000 thereafter, 
based on total proceeds; Bond Anticipation Notes – Base fee of $6,500 for each note plus $0.50 per 
$1,000 thereafter; Lease financings - $7,500; Environmental Facilities Corporation Financings plus 
$0.75 per $1,000 over $3,000,000.  The fee for general consulting services will be $150 per hour 
with the terms of the service agreed upon prior to the engagement. 
 
The fee for preparation and filing of the St atement of Annual Financial and Operating 
Information in accordance with SEC Rule 15c2-12, and the Town’s Continuing Disclosure 
Undertaking will be $2,600.  There is no charge for the filing of material event notices. 
 
All of the above fees represen t our experience in working w ith local governments for the 
last several years, a general understanding of the capital projec t plans and the necessary time 
expected to execute each transaction.  It should be noted that these fees represent a “not to exceed” 
amount.  Munistat has historically discounted much of the above fees  due to size, the issuance of 
more than one bond issue in a given year, or single-purpose borrowings. 
 
The fee for our services includes all out-of-p ocket expenses. Other normal issuing costs, 
such as bond counsel fees, rating agency fees and publication of resolutions and Notices of Sale are 
billed directly to the Town by the respective parties.  
 
Munistat Services, Inc. will not charge to a ttend meetings of the Board, work sessions, 
meetings with bond counsel, ratings agencies, or any other meetings asso ciated with a capital 
project.  We do not charge a ny fees for services delivered prior to a referendum, including 
preparation of estimated debt service and tax rate impact schedules.  There will be no charge until, 
and unless the closing of the bonds or notes take place.

Proposed Fee Schedule - 2026
Bonds
Our fee relating to a publicly sold serial bond issue would include a base charge of
$3,000 for the preparation of proceedings relating to the sale and issuance of the bonds. In
addition, we would charge a bond issuance fee of:
$0.85 per $1,000 for the first $5,000,000 financed
S.65 per $1,000 for the next $5,000,000 financed
S.45 per $1,000 thereafter.
The above calculation would be limited to a maximum of $10,000.
Notes
Our fee for publicly sold note issues, including bond anticipation notes, tax anticipation
notes, revenue anticipation notes, capital notes, budget notes and deficiency notes, whether
original or renewal issues, or issues which combine new obligations with renewal obligations,
would include a base charge of $700, plus a fee of:
$ .50 per $1,000 for the first $5,000,000 financed
S .40 per $1,000 for the next $5,000,000 financed
S .30 per $1,000 thereafter.
The above calculation would be limited to a maximum of $5,000.
Bonds Sold by Private Sale or Negotiated Sale
For statutory installment bonds or bonds sold by private sale or negotiated sale, the base
charge would be reduced to $1,500 and the maximum fee would be $5,000.
Bond Resolution Preparation Fees
Our fee for the preparation of bond resolutions would be $300 per bond resolution. Such
fee would generally be billed together with our fees for the initial serial bonds or bond
anticipation notes issued pursuant to such bond resolution(s).

§ 301-208. Redemption of preservation credits. [Amended 12-17-2024 by L.L. No. 43-2024] 
Preservation credits may be used to increase the development yields within mapped receiving 
areas according to the procedures set forth herein. In its determination of the total development

RA80 Zoning Use District utilizing preservation credits, the Planning Board shall condition 
the final map approval upon the redemption of the appropriate number of credits, with the 
signature and recording of the subdivision predicated upon the redemption and retiring of the 
appropriate number of preservation credits.  Preservation credits may also be used   for the 
development of golf cottages as accessory uses to standard golf courses, at a rate of one (1) 
golf cottage per preservation credit redeemed.

POLICY FOR RECEIPT OF DONATIONS 
 
 
I SCOPE 
 
This policy for receipt of donations applies to all donations, be it donated capital assets, 
contributions for purchase of capital assets, and short-term or non-capital assets made to the 
Town of Riverhead on behalf of any individual or other entity. 
 
 
II OBJECTIVES 
 
The primary objectives of the Town’s donation policy are: 
 
- To conform with all applicable Federal, State and other legal requirements 
- Establish policies and procedures for accepting donations, be it donated capital assets, 
contributions for purchase of capital assets, and short-term or non-capital assets 
- Identify donated contributions 
- To obtain and provide reasonable supporting documentation 
- To adequately safeguard the donation (use, misuse, obsolesce)  
- Determine and record the value of any donated assets; and 
- To adhere to any reporting requirements and properly reflect all donated assets in the 
financial statements.   
 
III AUTHORITY TO APPROVE DONATIONS  
 
The Town Board’s responsibility for administration of the donations policy is delegated to 
the Town Supervisor who shall establish written procedures for the operation of the donations 
policy consistent with all Federal and State applicable framework and guidelines. Such 
procedures shall include an adequate internal control structure to provide a satisfactory level of 
accountability based on a database or records incorporating descriptions and amounts of 
donations, transaction dates, value determination, and other relevant information that may be 
required and to regulate the activities of subordinate employees. The Donations Policy should 
be reviewed by all of the Town of Riverhead Departments as necessary. Any changes 
recommended should be approved by the Town Board and a Town Board Resolution. 
 
Acceptance of any donation will be authorized by Town Board Resolution and adopted 
with the Town Board’s vote pursuant to Town Law, § 64(8), the Town Board is expressly 
authorized to accept a gift “. . . for any public use, upon such terms or conditions as may be 
prescribed by the grantor or donor and accepted by said town, and provide for the proper 
administration of the same”; 
 
  
Page 211 of 252

IV PRUDENCE 
 
All participants in the donation process shall seek to act responsibly as custodians of the 
public trust and shall avoid any transactions that might impair public confidence in the Town of 
Riverhead to govern effectively. Any donation shall not conflict with any provisions of applicable 
Town Code, Local, State or Federal laws.  
 
The Town of Riverhead and the Town Board reserve the right to decline any donations 
without comment or cause. Any decision by the Town Board will be made with judgement and 
care, under circumstances then prevailing, which persons of prudence, discretion and 
intelligence exercised for the best interest of the Town.  
 
The Donor shall not expect, nor shall the Town grant, any extra consideration to the 
donor in relation to the Town’s procurement policies and procedures, regulatory matters, or any 
other business, services, application or permit processes in regard to the operations of the Town 
of Riverhead. Each Receipt of Donation Form will include a statement that no goods or services 
were provided by the Town of Riverhead in return for the donation. The Town of Riverhead has 
no obligation to return any donations as all donations are irrevocable and otherwise final upon 
receipt to the Town. The Town Board shall have the final authority to relocate, remove or dispose 
of any donation at any time, with or without notice to the donor. 
 
All participants involved in the donations policy shall refrain from personal business 
activity that could conflict with proper execution of the donations policy, or which could impair 
their ability to make impartial decisions.

Donated Capital Value Determination 
In accordance with the Internal Revenue Code, the Town of Riverhead does not provide 
an estimate value to donated capital assets and will not assign any monetary value except for 
cash donated capital assets. It is the responsibility of the donor to have any donated capital 
assets appraised before any contribution to the Town of Riverhead. Donors may refer to IRS 
Publication 561 for more information on valuing donated capital assets.  
For purpose of this policy, “cash or a monetary contribution” shall include the following 
types of payment sources: cash, check, a transfer of a gift card redeemable for cash, and a 
payment made by credit card, electronic fund transfer, an online payment service, or payroll 
deduction. 
For financial statement reporting purposes, the Town of Riverhead will follow the 
guidance provided by the Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) for generally 
accepted accounting principles for State and Local government municipalities.  
 
GASB Statement No. 72- Fair Value Measurement and Application provides guidance for 
determining a fair value to certain assets for financial reporting. This statement requires a 
government to use valuation techniques that are appropriate under the circumstances and for 
which sufficient data is available to measure fair value.   
 
For donated capital assets, this statement requires that the asset be recorded at 
acquisition value. The acquisition value is the price that would be paid to acquire an equivalent 
operational unit on the government’s principal market or a government’s most advantageous 
market in absence of a principal market at that date of the transaction occurrence. The date of 
contribution is the date on which any assets are delivered to the Town of Riverhead or the title 
transfer date, whereas the donor no longer retains any rights to or interest in the property that 
would limit the Town’s use of the donated capital assets. 
 
In some instances when sufficient data is not available or in which the acquisition value 
may be too complex to ascertain, the Town of Riverhead would be able to use the donor’s cost 
basis, of cost incurred or an internal cost estimate calculation based on the most advantageous 
prices of similar assets or infrastructure as a measurement of donated capital assets value.

Recording of Donated Capital Assets 
Governmental Funds- The Town's governmental funds are currently accounted for on a 
modified accrual basis. Based on guidance from the Office of the New York State Comptroller 
and the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report, donated capital assets when received by a 
governmental fund will not record a financial transaction. However, the donated capital asset 
and the corresponding deprecation is recorded in the Government Wide financial statements or 
Schedule K of Non-Current Government Assets.  
 
Proprietary Funds (Water and Sewer Districts)- The Town's proprietary funds are 
accounted for on a full accrual basis. When donated capital assets are received by a proprietary 
fund, they will be recorded as a financial transaction in the fund’s financial statements and 
capitalized for deprecation.  
 
Donated capital assets to the Town of Riverhead will be depreciated or amortized 
consistent with the Town’s Capital and Intangible Asset Capitalization policies. The Town will 
use the related useful lives based on the type of asset and utilize the date of acceptance by the 
Town of Riverhead and Town Board as the acquisition date.

retention policy and shall comply with all reporting requirements and regulations including, but

requirements of the New York State Office of the Comptroller.

ARTICLE VI 
 
GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE 
 
1.  Consideration of Grievance. 
 
A grievance by Cote shall be made, in writing, to the Town Board.  Upon receipt of the 
grievance, the Town Board may request Cote  to submit any agreed statement of facts or 
his/her version of the facts, or any other documents that the Town Board may deem 
pertinent to the determination of the appeal. 
 
The Town Board shall conduct a hearing within twenty (20) business days of receipt of an 
appeal.  Within twent y (20) business days after the hearing, the Town Board  shall make a 
decision based on its findings and advise Cote.  The decision of the Town Board shall be 
final and all parties bound thereby. 
 
2.  Time of Hearings. 
 
All discussions and hearings shall, so far as practicable, be conducted during working 
hours.
